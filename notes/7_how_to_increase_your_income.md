# Chapter 7 – How to Increase Your Income

## Chapter Summary
This chapter shifts focus from saving and debt elimination to income expansion. Schäfer argues that wealth creation depends more on increasing earning capacity than on reducing expenses. Income growth is driven by personal performance, initiative, discipline, skill development, and strategic positioning. The core message: financial ceilings are usually self-imposed.

---

## Paragraph 7.1 – Show Strength
### Summary
Professional strength is demonstrated through reliability, decisiveness, and resilience.

### Key Ideas
- Strength builds trust.  
- Employers reward stability and competence.  
- Confidence influences perception.

### Practical Strategies
- Deliver consistently under pressure.  
- Make decisions instead of avoiding them.  
- Maintain composure during setbacks.

### Notes / Observations
- Perceived strength increases professional value.

---

## Paragraph 7.2 – Ask Yourself About Your Duties, Not Your Rights
### Summary
Focusing on contribution rather than entitlement increases income potential.

### Key Ideas
- Value creation precedes compensation.  
- Entitlement limits growth.  
- Responsibility increases opportunity.

### Practical Strategies
- Identify how you can exceed expectations.  
- Measure your contribution objectively.  
- Prioritize performance over complaints.

### Notes / Observations
- Income correlates with value delivered.

---

## Paragraph 7.3 – If You’re Paid for Eight Hours, Work Ten
### Summary
Going beyond minimum requirements differentiates you from average performers.

### Key Ideas

- Short-term overdelivery creates reputation capital.  
- Competitive markets reward intensity.

### Practical Strategies
- Invest additional time in skill refinement.  
- Focus on high-impact tasks beyond obligations.  
- Build a reputation for exceeding standards.

### Notes / Observations
- Temporary overcommitment can accelerate advancement.

## Chapter Conclusions
- Increasing income is more powerful than cutting expenses.  
- Contribution, initiative, and discipline differentiate high earners.  
- Speed and reliability enhance perceived value.  
- Continuous education expands earning potential.  
- Sustainable income growth requires personal evolution.
